# About

I’m a thesis-based Master’s student at **McGill University**, graduating **May–June 2026**, focused on building reliable data-driven systems. I enjoy collaborative environments where I can learn from others, contribute clearly, and take ownership of my part of the work—from understanding requirements and designing systems to validating results and improving performance. I love working with data because it tells stories that numbers alone can’t, and I’m motivated by building solutions that make decisions smarter and workflows easier.

I spent three years as a **Big Data Developer** in the **Anti Money Laundering (AML)** domain at **American Express (via TCS)**. I built batch ingestion pipelines and automated reporting workflows using **SQL, Hive, Spark, Python, and Unix shell**, working with large, sensitive datasets across fraud, risk, customer, and merchant domains. I owned two major reports and partnered closely with analysts and engineers to deliver accurate outputs, improve reliability, and optimize performance.

In my thesis, I focus on extracting statistically reliable patterns from large DevOps datasets using two approaches:  
- **LLM-based pattern discovery** with structured prompting and evaluation  
- **Statistical pattern mining** using **FP-Growth** for high-confidence results  

To make findings trustworthy and scalable, I built an automated **pattern validator tool** using a domain-specific language and **model-to-text code generation** that produces **Java validators**. I tend to lean toward logically correct solutions, keep my perspectives open to new evidence, and avoid bias in decision-making.

I’m actively looking for roles in **data engineering, data science, AI agent, and applied ML**, and I’m open to varied domains where data quality, automation, and measurable impact matter. Outside of work and research, I enjoy connecting with people, sharing knowledge, and learning from different perspectives.
